A Brief History of Me

A

s a kid in the 90s when the internet was just picking up speed I grew up with the web in my blood. As a kid, I fiddled with code to make my Neopets shop the prettiest and coolest of them all. And in high school I had the honor of redesigning the school's website which led to more website commissions from school clubs. Because I loved building web experiences I made the decision to study Computer Science in university so I could better understand the underworkings of programming and the web.

In my sophomore year at university I landed a position as a technical assistant for a new research center and a webmaster position for a small chemistry club, helping both groups build and maintain a new website. In my junior year I was hired as a web developer for the Daily Bruin and the student development group BuildUCLA design to build web pages and mobile applications. All these positions helped me build team experience, taught me a lot of valuable new skills while building on the old, and allowed me to share my passion for the web by teaching others.

After four years of late nights and tough competitions I graduated in 2015, and now I'm back home in the San Francisco Bay Area looking for more work designing and building awesome web experiences and continuing to learn more and more about the work I love so much.
